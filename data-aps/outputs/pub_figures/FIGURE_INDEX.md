# Publication Figures — Index

**These are the final figures for the paper.** All other figure directories (`figures/`, `figures_lowseed/`) are intermediate outputs from individual scripts and do not need review.

## Structural Motivation (Section 4)

| File | What it shows |
|---|---|
| `fig1_degree_distributions.png` | In-degree vs out-degree distributions of the APS citation graph. In-degree is heavy-tailed; out-degree (bibliography length) is bounded. Motivates why forward traversal explodes and backward traversal stays local. |
| `fig2_bfs_reachability.png` | BFS reachability from the three survey papers seeded directly. Shows that bidirectional traversal is necessary — forward-only misses much of the gold set, backward-only misses later work. |
| `fig3_strategy_comparison.png` | Recall vs corpus size for four traversal strategies: backward, forward, bidirectional, and Pareto-filtered bidirectional. Shows the Pareto filter keeps corpus small while preserving recall. |
| `fig4_screen_yield_collapse.png` | Screen yield (gold papers found / papers visited) by depth, across surveys. Yield collapses sharply at depth 2–3, confirming the stopping criterion is principled. |
| `fig8_efficiency_frontier.png` | Recall vs screened corpus size across Pareto threshold values (50/70/80/90/100%). The Pareto-80 operating point recovers 89–98% of gold sets at manageable corpus size. |

## Main Results (Section 5)

| File | What it shows |
|---|---|
| `fig5_cold_start_recall_per_round.png` | Recall after round 1 and round 2, at k=5 top-k seeds, for all three surveys. Compares how much round 2 adds over round 1. |
| `fig6_recall_vs_seed_size.png` | Recall (round 2) vs seed size k ∈ {1,2,3,4,5,10} for top-k, random, and contaminated seeds, across all surveys. Shows saturation pattern and seed-quality robustness. |

## Miss Analysis + Trade-off Frontier (Section 6)

| File | What it shows |
|---|---|
| `fig7_miss_analysis.png` | Structural properties of unrecovered papers vs recovered: in-degree distribution, BFS distance from recovered set, year/journal breakdown. Supports the structural-periphery interpretation. |
| `fig9a_pareto_yield_heatmap.png` | Heatmap of final recall over Pareto threshold × yield threshold. Shows where the operating point sits in parameter space. |
| `fig9b_recall_vs_pareto.png` | Recall vs Pareto threshold for each survey, at fixed yield threshold. The full trade-off frontier for hub suppression. |

| `fig9d_corpus_size_heatmap.png` | Corpus size (screening cost) over Pareto × yield threshold. Pairs with fig9a to show the recall–cost trade-off. |

## Depth × Pareto Grid (Section 6 supplement)

| File | What it shows |
|---|---|
| `fig8b_depth_pareto_heatmap.png` | Recall (recall_refs) at every (BFS depth, Pareto-percentile threshold) combination, shown as annotated heatmaps for all three surveys. Rows = depths 1–6; columns = Pareto thresholds 10/20/30/40/50/70/80/90/None (no filter). Key finding: at full depth (6) all settings reach 100% recall, but at low depth (2) aggressive filtering (pareto10) drops recall to 31–53%. Pareto-80 at depth 2 already recovers 85–98%. Raw grid data in `traversal_results_depth_pareto_grid.json`. Generated by `analysis-scripts/03b_depth_pareto_grid.py`. |
